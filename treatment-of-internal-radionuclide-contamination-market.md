<p><strong>The Treatment of Internal Radionuclide Contamination Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Potassium Iodide</li><li>Prussian Blue</li><li>Diethylenetriamine Pentaacetic Acid</li><li>DMSA</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The treatment of internal radionuclide contamination involves various pharmacological agents. Potassium Iodide is used to block radioactive iodine absorption in the thyroid. Prussian Blue acts as a chelating agent, facilitating the excretion of cesium and thallium. Diethylenetriamine Pentaacetic Acid (DTPA) is effective for removing plutonium, americium, and curium by binding to them and enhancing excretion. Dimercaptosuccinic Acid (DMSA) is utilized to treat lead and mercury contamination. Other treatments may include supportive care and additional chelating agents, depending on the specific radionuclide involved.</p></p>
