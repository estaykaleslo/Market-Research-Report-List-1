<p><ul><li>Amorphous Silicon Technology</li><li>Vanadium Oxide Technology</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The uncooled infrared (IR) detector chip market is primarily characterized by two types of technologies: amorphous silicon and vanadium oxide. Amorphous silicon technology utilizes a thin film structure to convert IR radiation into an electrical signal, offering benefits like low cost and good sensitivity. In contrast, vanadium oxide technology employs a semiconductor material that provides excellent thermal sensitivity and faster response times, making it suitable for a range of applications. Both technologies are pivotal in driving advancements in thermal imaging and various sensing applications.</p></p>
